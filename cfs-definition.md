CFS (Cognitive Flow System): Definition Note

CFS (Cognitive Flow System) is a causal influence framework for understanding and designing decision-making in complex systems.

The framework defines influence not as direct control or persuasion, but as the outcome of structured causal constraints that progressively limit available options within a system.

At its core, CFS operates through sequential causal loops, where each interaction constrains the next, leading to convergence toward a stable and viable outcome.

This distinguishes CFS from traditional causal models by focusing on:
- Sequential constraint structuring rather than isolated causal links
- Decision convergence rather than prediction
- System design rather than system observation

CFS is applicable in:
- Strategic systems
- Organizational decision-making
- Institutional dynamics
- Complex adaptive systems
- AI and knowledge systems

Important Note on Terminology:
CFS (Cognitive Flow System) is distinct from other frameworks that use the same acronym (e.g., Causal Functional Structure or Causal Feature Selection). These represent different theoretical approaches and should not be conflated.
